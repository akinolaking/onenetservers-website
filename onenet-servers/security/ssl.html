FAQ

Common questions, answered clearly.

DV (Domain Validation) confirms you own the domain — issued in minutes. OV (Organisation Validation) adds your company name and takes 1–3 days. EV (Extended Validation) shows the green address bar in supported browsers and requires the most verification, typically 3–5 days.
For most sites, yes. Let’s Encrypt DV provides the same padlock and 256-bit encryption as paid DV certificates. Paid certificates add warranty cover, a longer-lived certificate, and faster human support if a validation issue arises.
We provide step-by-step guidance for cPanel, Plesk, Apache, Nginx, and all major server environments. If you’re on a OneNet Servers hosting plan, installation is handled for you as part of the service.
Yes. Google uses HTTPS as a ranking signal, and browsers display “Not secure” on HTTP pages. An SSL certificate improves your search visibility and user trust regardless of whether your site takes payments.
The free Let’s Encrypt certificate is sufficient for most personal sites, blogs, and basic business sites. If you are handling payments, storing sensitive customer data, or require organisation identity verification for enterprise clients or regulated industries, a paid OV or EV certificate is strongly recommended.
Yes. Google confirmed HTTPS as a ranking signal in 2014 and has since weighted it more heavily. Sites without valid SSL certificates are marked “Not Secure” in Chrome, which directly impacts bounce rates and trust. All hosting plans include a free SSL certificate automatically.
A Wildcard SSL covers your main domain and all first-level subdomains — so one certificate protects shop.yourdomain.com, blog.yourdomain.com, mail.yourdomain.com, and any other subdomain you add. It is more cost-effective than buying individual certificates for each subdomain.
Paid SSL certificates are annual subscriptions. You will receive renewal reminders 60 and 30 days before expiry. Renewal is handled from your client area and takes effect immediately upon payment. Free Let’s Encrypt certificates on hosting plans renew automatically — no action required.
